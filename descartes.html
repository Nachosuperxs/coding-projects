Descartes

The father of modern philosophy


Facts about Descartes:


  • Descartes came up with the famous phrase "Cogito Ergo Sum" meaning "I am thinking, therefore, I am"

  • Descartes used his methodological doubt in order to doubt all that he knew in his Meditations.

  • The "Meditations on First Philosophy" became his most famous work, and it is still studied by philosophers.

  • Descartes created the graphing and plotting in coordinates system, hence the Cartesian plane.

  • As Descartes was called a rationalist, his philosophy opposed the various ideas proposed by empiricists.

  • He is often made fun of for proposing that the pineal gland was the organ that connected the body to the soul.

  • Descartes didn't think animals had souls and once notoriously dissected a dog alive.

Despite all of the oddities in his life, his work changed philosophy and the entire world. This page was built as a tribute for him.
